Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Learn about Safety Rights, Education Rights, and Health Rights

Safety Rights

What should you do if a stranger tries to talk to you?
You should stay away from the stranger and find a trusted adult or a safe place.
What can you do if you feel unsafe at school?
You should report it to a teacher, counselor, or another trusted adult at school.
How can you protect your privacy online?
You should avoid sharing personal information online and only communicate with trusted people.
What should you do if someone is bullying you?
You should immediately tell a teacher, counselor, or trusted adult to stop the bullying.
Is it okay to open the door for strangers at home?
No, it is important to ask who it is before opening the door and only let trusted people in.

Education Rights

What is the right of every child in terms of education?
Every child has the right to free and compulsory education up to a certain age.
At what age should a child start going to school?
Children should start attending school around the age of 6.
Can education be denied to a child?
No, it is illegal to deny a child their right to education in many countries.
What should a child do if they can't afford education materials?
A child should ask for help from school authorities or apply for educational assistance programs.
Do children have the right to study in their mother tongue?
Yes, children have the right to access education in their native language or mother tongue.

Health Rights

What is the right of a child regarding health care?
Every child has the right to access health care services, including vaccination, treatment, and preventive care.
How should children be protected from harmful substances?
Children should be protected from harmful chemicals and substances through regulations and awareness campaigns.
What should a parent do if their child needs medical attention but can't afford it?
They should contact social services or healthcare assistance programs that can provide support.
Is it necessary for children to have regular check-ups?
Yes, regular health check-ups are important to monitor a child's growth and development.
Can children refuse medical treatment?
In many cases, children may have the right to refuse certain medical treatments, depending on their age and the laws in their country.
